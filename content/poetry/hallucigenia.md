---
title: "Hallucigenia"
slug: hallucigenia
weight: 4
---

The first thing they got wrong was which way up.\
Seven pairs of spines, mistaken for legs —\
the stilted walk of something that could not\
have walked that way, parading through\
the textbooks on invented feet. The actual legs, soft-lobed\
and delicate, got read as tentacles\
along the back, waving at nothing,\
ornamental. For decades it stood\
on what protected it\
and waved with what it walked on.

Then they flipped it. Turned the head around.\
The old reconstruction — filed, referenced,\
illustrated in a dozen children's books\
with confident bright colors — was not\
the animal. Was not even close.\
Just an arrangement someone made\
from the impression of a body\
pressed five hundred million years ago\
into the silt floor of a shallow sea,\
each soft part dissolved, each spine\
a needle held in negative,\
in stone.

They found the eyes eventually — dark stains\
the size of periods, clustered near\
what had been designated tail.\
The teeth, when they were found, were rings —\
a throat lined with tiny circles,\
opening and closing\
on whatever passed for food\
in the Middle Cambrian.\
We don't know how it bred. We know\
the turbidity current hit, the cloud\
of sediment that killed and kept it\
in the same blind stroke.

What we know is the impression.\
The animal is inference.\
Every version of it\
is a scaffold built from absence\
and the names we give\
to shapes we almost recognize —\
*Hallucigenia*, because it looked\
like nothing else, because the namer\
reached for the least certain word he had.\
Not monster. Not miracle. Hallucination:\
a thing the mind produces\
when the evidence fails to resolve.
