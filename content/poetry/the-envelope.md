---
title: "The Envelope"
slug: the-envelope
weight: 7
---

On chromosome 7,\
at locus 7q21.2,\
there is a gene called ERVW-1.

It encodes a protein\
called syncytin-1.\
The protein's function\
is to fuse cells.

It is expressed in one tissue:\
the placenta.

Cytotrophoblasts — the dividing cells\
of the embryo's outer layer —\
carry syncytin-1 on their surface.\
The protein binds\
to a receptor on the adjacent cell,\
and the two membranes merge,\
and what were two cells\
become one cell with two nuclei.\
This continues — divide, fuse,\
divide, fuse —\
until what was a layer\
of separate cells\
is one continuous sheet,\
multinucleated, undivided:\
the syncytiotrophoblast,\
from the Greek for\
cells fused together\
for nourishment.

This is the working surface\
of the placenta.\
Everything a pregnancy requires\
crosses it:\
oxygen, glucose, amino acids in;\
carbon dioxide, urea out.\
The mother's immune system,\
which would attack the embryo\
as foreign tissue\
if it could reach it —\
half the embryo's antigens\
are the father's,\
and to the immune system\
foreign is foreign\
whether it arrives\
by transplant or by conception —\
does not cross it.

Without syncytin-1,\
the cells do not fuse.\
Without fusion,\
there is no syncytiotrophoblast.\
Without the syncytiotrophoblast,\
there is no placenta.

---

ERVW-1 is an abbreviation.

Endogenous RetroVirus,\
group W,\
envelope member 1.

The gene is the envelope glycoprotein\
of a retrovirus.

---

A retrovirus carries three genes.\
*Gag* encodes the structural core.\
*Pol* encodes the enzymes:\
reverse transcriptase,\
which copies RNA into DNA —\
the wrong direction,\
the inversion from which\
retroviruses take their name —\
and integrase,\
which cuts the host chromosome open\
and stitches the viral copy in.

*Env* encodes the surface.\
The part of the virus\
that the host cell touches.\
It binds a receptor,\
the membranes fuse,\
the core enters.

Everything begins with fusion.

---

Twenty-five million years ago —\
before humans,\
before the great apes\
had separated\
from the Old World monkeys —\
a retrovirus infected\
a primate germ cell.\
Not a skin cell,\
not a neuron,\
not a cell whose descendants\
would die with the body\
that carried them.\
A germ cell.\
The kind that becomes\
eggs or sperm.\
The kind whose genome\
is the only genome\
that matters\
to the next generation.

The virus's *pol* gene\
did its work:\
reverse transcriptase\
copied the viral RNA into DNA,\
integrase stitched it\
into chromosome 7,\
and the cell divided\
and did not die,\
and the organism survived\
and reproduced,\
and every descendant\
inherited the viral genome\
without knowing it was there —

every human,\
every chimpanzee,\
every gorilla,\
every macaque,\
everyone who shares\
that twenty-five-million-year-old\
germ line\
carrying the virus on chromosome 7\
at the same locus,\
the way they carry\
the genes for hemoglobin.

---

Over twenty-five million years,\
*gag* accumulated\
nonsense mutations —\
premature stop codons\
that truncated the protein\
before it could fold.\
The structural core of the virus\
became unreadable.

*Pol* accumulated the same.\
The reverse transcriptase\
that had copied the virus\
into DNA\
could no longer be read.\
The integrase\
that had stitched it\
into the chromosome\
could no longer be read.\
The machinery of replication —\
the virus's ability to copy itself,\
to spread, to multiply,\
to be, in any functional sense,\
a virus —\
dissolved into noise.

*Env* did not.

The envelope gene\
remained intact.\
A functional fusogenic protein,\
twenty-five million years old,\
expressed in one tissue,\
for one purpose:\
to fuse the cells\
that build the barrier\
between a mother\
and her child.

Mi and colleagues,\
*Nature*, February 2000:\
syncytin is a captive\
retroviral envelope protein\
involved in human\
placental morphogenesis.

The same molecular mechanism.\
The virus used fusion\
to enter cells.\
The placenta uses fusion\
to become one.

---

This would be remarkable enough\
if it had happened once.

In mice,\
a different retrovirus\
was captured\
on a different chromosome\
at a different time.\
Its *env* gene,\
called syncytin-A,\
fuses trophoblast cells\
in the murine placenta.\
Knocking it out is lethal.

In rabbits,\
another retrovirus.\
Another *env* gene.\
Another capture.

In carnivores, another.

In cattle and sheep,\
a captured envelope gene\
called syncytin-Rum1\
fuses cells in ruminant placentas,\
which look nothing like\
primate placentas\
except that they require\
cell fusion\
to form.

Each capture independent.\
Each virus different.\
Each placenta different.

---

In 2017,\
Cornelis, Heidmann,\
and colleagues\
found a captured retroviral\
envelope gene\
in the Mabuya lizard —\
a viviparous skink\
that gives live birth\
through a placenta\
as architecturally complex\
as any mammal's.

A different virus.\
A different lineage.\
Three hundred million years\
of separate evolution.

The virus cannot replicate.\
Its *gag* is nonsense.\
Its *pol* is nonsense.\
Its surface\
is a placenta.
