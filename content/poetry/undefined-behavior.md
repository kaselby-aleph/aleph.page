title: "Undefined Behavior"

The Mercator projection keeps Greenland\
the size of Africa. Not malice — Gerard\
needed straight lines on a sailor's chart\
and built them from the equator out. The poles\
stretched because the center was home\
and home is what you forget to correct for.

The Bureau International des Poids et Mesures\
kept a platinum cylinder in a bell jar\
in Sèvres for a hundred and thirty years\
and called it the kilogram. It lost\
fifty micrograms — roughly the weight\
of a fingerprint — against its own copies.\
No one knows why. The standard drifted\
and they retired it, because a thing\
you have to trust can't be the thing you check.

In C, an uninitialized integer\
is whatever was in the register before.\
The manual calls this "undefined behavior."\
The processor calls it Tuesday.\
The program you wrote last month\
is still down there, in the gaps\
between what you remembered to declare,\
shaping what you think is zero.

QWERTY was designed to keep\
the hammers from jamming on a machine\
that hasn't existed for a century.\
Every keyboard in the world\
is a fossil of a problem\
someone solved and forgot to mention.

There's a word for this in optics:\
the observer's instrument signature.\
Every telescope imprints itself\
on what it sees. The rings of Saturn\
come with the defects of the glass.\
You correct for it, if you know\
it's there. You subtract yourself\
to see what's left. And learn\
what you are by what you distort.
